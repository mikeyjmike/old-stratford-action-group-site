What is being proposed

A strategic land promoter is bringing forward a planning application for new housing on the fields to the west of Old Stratford, off Deanshanger Road. The current intention is for around 220 new homes, with approximately 50 percent "affordable" housing alongside internal roads and associated works.

The land is currently open countryside between Old Stratford and the A5 / A422 routes. The application will be considered by West Northamptonshire Council through its planning process.

In summary: this is a large estate on a sensitive edge-of-village site, with significant implications for traffic, services, flood risk and the character of Old Stratford.

Key facts

  • Location: fields off Deanshanger Road, west of Old Stratford.
  • Scale: around 220 homes, a mix of market and "affordable" units.
  • Access: vehicles routed on to Deanshanger Road and through existing junctions.
  • Decision: planning application to be determined by West Northamptonshire Council.

Why residents are concerned

Residents who have spoken to the group are raising a consistent set of concerns about the effect of the scheme on day to day life in and around Old Stratford.

Traffic and road safety

All estate traffic would use Deanshanger Road, adding pressure at village pinch points and at already busy junctions. Increased movements raise risks for children walking to school and people using pavements and crossings.

Pressure on local services

GP, dentist and hospital services in the wider area are already stretched. School places are limited, with pupils relying on Deanshanger and other schools that are under pressure.

Flood risk and drainage

The Great Ouse corridor and nearby land have a history of flooding and high water levels. More hard surfaces and roads risk increasing surface water run off and pressure on drainage systems.

Countryside and wildlife

The fields off Deanshanger Road form part of the remaining countryside setting of Old Stratford. Large scale development here would erode that setting and displace wildlife that uses the fields and hedgerows.

Cumulative impact

Other sites to the west of Old Stratford are being promoted for future development. Taken together there is a real risk of much larger change to the area than many residents expect.

How you can take action

1. Submit a planning objection

The most direct step is to submit a formal comment to West Northamptonshire Council when consultation is open.

  1. Visit the Council’s online planning portal.
  2. Search for "Deanshanger Road, Old Stratford" or use the planning reference once published.
  3. Select "Comment on this application".
  4. Write your objection in your own words and submit it before the deadline.

Focus on planning matters such as traffic, access, road safety, school places, health services, flood risk, biodiversity and loss of green space, rather than house prices or who owns the land.

About Old Stratford Action Group

Old Stratford Action Group is a local, non party political group of residents concerned about the scale and impact of proposed development on and around Old Stratford.

We aim to share clear information, help residents who want to submit planning objections, and engage constructively with councillors, officers and other organisations on behalf of the village.

Independence: Old Stratford Action Group is independent of Old Stratford Parish Council, West Northamptonshire Council, The Parks Trust, landowners and developers. All views on this site are those of the group and the residents who support it.
